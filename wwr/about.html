History

Founded in 1999, White Water Rafting has been providing unforgettable experiences. In the misty dawn of civilization, the river's gentle voice whispered secrets to ancient ears. The Egyptians, Greeks, and Romans listened, crafting rafts to tame the currents and harness the power of the water. Transportation, trade, and exploration â€“ the river's bounty was theirs to claim. Centuries passed, and the river's siren call beckoned explorers to uncharted shores. Francisco Pizarro and John Wesley Powell answered, their rafts bucking the rapids as they charted the unknown. The Colorado River's mighty waters trembled beneath Powell's oars in 1869, a historic descent that etched his name in the annals of time. The 20th century dawned, and the river's allure transformed. In the 1960s, adventurers discovered inflatable rafts, safety gear, and the thrill of whitewater. Bill Dvorak and John "Dutch" Schultz pioneered commercial rafting, sharing the river's magic with the world. As the sport gained momentum, the International Rafting Federation (IRF) took shape in 1997, uniting enthusiasts globally. The river's voice grew louder, drawing thrill-seekers to iconic destinations: the Grand Canyon, Snake River, Amazon River, and Zambezi River. Today, rafting's evolution continues, driven by innovation and passion. The river's call remains irresistible, beckoning all who dare to ride its waves.
